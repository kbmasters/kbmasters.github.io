Men's health: important factors affecting sperm quality

Men's health: important factors affecting sperm quality

Male fertility is directly related to the quality of sperm. For this reason, one of the first tests that is prescribed for difficulties with conception is spermogramma.

The cycle of sperm production is long, so many factors can affect the quality of sperm. The duration of the cycle occurs on average within 100 days. The division and development of cells in the testes takes about 75 days; 20 days to pass through the appendage of the testis and about 5 days to pass through the testes through the VAS deferens.

During these approximately 100 days, the fluid is vulnerable to environmental factors, nutrition, individual operating mode, and much more.

Handling and contact with toxic substances, pesticides, chemicals, ethylene oxide, vinyl chloride, heavy metals, lead, cadmium, mercury, radioactive elements, x-rays and other pollutants have a direct impact on the development of spermatozoa.

Smoking, excessive consumption of alcohol, coffee, stimulants and drugs have serious consequences. It has been shown that they are directly damage the cells from which the liquid originates (spermogonia). Nicotine and tar adversely affect the fertility of spermatozoa, mainly on their concentration, mobility and morphology. To this, it can be added that Smoking is associated with poor sperm function during penetration, and also leads to an increase in white blood cells in the ejaculate.

Prolonged driving and Cycling, wearing tight pants and underpants (instead of "family shirts"), taking hot baths, showers and saunas, as well as any exposure to high temperatures on the genitals lead to an increase in the temperature of the testicles. In turn, heat strongly disrupts the process of fission and maturation of gametes.

Adverse factors include fatigue, mental strain, sedentary lifestyle, overweight, unhealthy diet, prolonged computer use, and stress.

Even slightly elevated temperature (by 2 degrees) may negatively affect education sperms'. Their concentration will decrease, the number of healthy ones will decrease, and the number of damaged ones will increase.

In the summer season, the concentration of spermatozoa decreases. Thus, the sperm count in the ejaculate can fall from 320 million to 250 million.

Warm bath (43-45 degrees) on the half an hour a day significantly reduces the concentration of sperm. The same effect can be found in tight underwear, sports shorts or trousers.
