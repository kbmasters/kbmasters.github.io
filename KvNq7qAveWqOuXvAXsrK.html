The most common human fears

The most common human fears

Fear is an emotion that protects us from danger. Scientists believe that this emotion is even stronger than love, because our survival depends on fear. It is formed in the most primitive part of the brain associated with anxiety and avoidance of dangerous situations.

Fear is accompanied by some physical symptoms, in particular a rapid heartbeat, rapid breathing, at which time blood is sent to the muscles, adrenaline enters the bloodstream, and the body prepares for a "fight or flight" situation. in civilized countries, people are less likely to encounter such situations, but there are many other new fears that cause similar changes in the body. Below are some of the most common human fears of modern times.

Fear of losing personal freedom.

There is no exact definition the term "freedom" is used, and its value is defined differently in each society. But the fear of losing your freedom has existed since ancient times, although it is not something we think about every day. This fear can be caused, for example, when you are locked in your room without the right to go outside until you finish your homework or until the quarantine is lifted.

Fear of success.

For some people who have experienced physical or mental abuse in the past, the pleasant feeling that accompanies a successful case can lead to discomfort and somehow remind them of their traumatic past experiences. This makes them avoid such joyful situations in order to feel safe and secure.

Fear of failure.

The fear of failure is sometimes so paralyzing that it even prevents a person from achieving their goals. People who suffer from this fear tend to underestimate their abilities and efforts, thus protecting yourself from disappointment. Many people who grew up with very strict and demanding parents suffer from this fear.

Fear of failure can serve as a catalyst for triggering certain mental illnesses, such as generalized anxiety disorder, depression, or obsessive-compulsive disorder disorder.

Fear of death.

Thanatophobia is an abnormal and constant fear of death, which is often accompanied by a sense of anxiety. Elderly people who suffer from physical or psychological problems may experience severe anxiety associated with the fear of imminent death. Sometimes the fear the fear of the future is so strong that people are not able to live life to the full.

Fear of attachment and intimacy.

Approximately 17% of adults suffer from fear of intimacy and creating close relationships. Most often, these people in childhood had a bad relationship with their parents, which they were not able to create an environment of comfort and security.

When a child is unable to Express his emotions freely and receives intolerance or neglect from his parents, he forms a childhood trauma. As soon as he begins to grow closer to another person in adulthood, the traumas experienced in childhood do not allow him to create a relationship with another person. a full emotional connection.

Fear of spiders.

Arachnophobia is an irrational fear of spiders. This is one of the oldest and most well-studied human fears. Some scientists believe that it was formed a long time ago, and its purpose is to protect us. All because with the bite of many spiders they are poisonous and can cause infection and subsequent death. Researchers found that approximately 32% of women and 18% of men suffer from arachnophobia.

Fear of public speaking.

Glossophobia is the fear of public speaking. Usually, people who suffer from this phobia experience strong anxiety, which can lead to it can turn into a panic before a public speech. Such anxiety can be accompanied by nausea, vomiting, rapid heartbeat and high blood pressure, dry mouth and muscle tension.
